Mac and cheese

Description

Macaroni and cheese is a comforting dish made with tender pasta coated in a creamy, rich cheese sauce, often baked until golden and bubbly. It’s a classic, satisfying meal perfect for any cheese lover.

Ingredients for macaroni and cheese

For the cheese sauce

  • Butter
  • All-purpose floor
  • Milk
  • Cheddar cheese, shredded
  • Gruyère cheese, shredded (optional, for extra richness)
  • Cream cheese
  • Salt and pepper
  • Mustard powder (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Paprika or cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

For the pasta

  • Elbow macaroni (or any other small pasta shape)

Optional toppings:

  • Breadcrumbs
  • Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Steps to make macaroni and cheese

Cook the pasta

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil
  2. Add the pasta to the water and cook according to packages instructions Until al dente
  3. Drain and set aside

Prepare the cheese sauce

  1. In a large saucepan, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat.
  2. Add 4 tablespoons of flour and whisk continously to form a smooth paste. Cook for about 1-2 minutes without browning.
  3. Gradually add 4 cups of milk, whisking continously to avoid lumps.
  4. Add salt, pepper and optional seasoning like mustard powder, paprika or cayenne pepper.
  5. Gradually add the shredded cheese and gruyère, stir until fully melted and the sauce is smooth.
  6. You can also add cream cheese for extra creaminess.

Combine pasta and cheese sauce

  1. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce.
  2. Stir until the pasta are evenly coated.

Bake the maraconi and cheese (Optional)

  1. Preheat the over to 175°C
  2. Pour the macaroni and cheese into a greased baking dish.
  3. If desired, sprinkle the top with breadcrumbs mixed with grated parmesan.
  4. Bake in the preheated over for about 20-25 minutes or until the top is golden and bubbly.

Serve

  1. If desired, sprinkle with fresh parsley.
  2. Serve hot and enjoy your creamy, cheesy macaroni and cheese.
